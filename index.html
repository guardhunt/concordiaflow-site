Presence Coaching

Build Body-Based Awareness.
Arrive with Presence.

What kept you safe once may now be holding you back. ConcordiaSpring works to gently release what no longer serves you, and return to the aliveness, connection, and confidence that was always yours.

What this work is

Most of us carry patterns we didn't consciously choose: ways of bracing, withdrawing, overextending, or going numb that once made sense. These are not failures of willpower. They're intelligent adaptations to difficult experiences, moving not just in our thoughts or emotions, but also in our bodies.

ConcordiaSpring works at the level where those patterns live: in the nervous system, in the breath, in the felt sense of the body, and in the relational space between us.

We engage in a practice of presence, building familiarity and trust with our bodies and their wisdom, allowing our experiences to be the teachers of our own transformation.

"Ease returning to places of tension. Connection returning to places of loneliness. Empowerment returning to places that feel stuck."

How we work together

Learning from Nature

"There is an intelligence in the system that distributes nourishment according to need, that links strength to fragility without hierarchy or resistance.

A human system aligned with presence does not hoard ease and reject discomfort. It allows experience to circulate. What is strong supports what is tender. What is clear meets what is confused. Nothing is cut off.

The energy produced in moments of openness moves through the system and feeds the places that are still contracted. This exchange increases resilience, not by eliminating stress, but by integrating it into a larger coherence.

Stress in a closed, individual system leads to disregulation and even destruction. Stress in an interconnected system becomes a single signal within a larger conversion that rebalances resources and shifts energy in a caring and responsive manner."

You belong to yourself.

ConcordiaSpring is an invitation back into your own life, into full presence, full agency, and the kind of coherence that's possible when you're no longer at war with yourself.

Immersions

Finding membership in the body of life

Immersions are guided, small-group experiences blending somatic movement, breathwork, stillness, and shared presence. Set in the forests and trails around Asheville, they offer a gentle space to return to your body, your breath, and the earth — together. No experience is necessary — just bring yourself, comfortable shoes, and an openness to presence.

Each immersion is slow-paced with frequent pauses, including simple practices like grounding, breathing, and noticing. We move in and out of silence, sometimes sharing reflections, sometimes just walking side by side. It’s a space for realness — to feel, to rest, to reconnect with the ecology of your own body and the living world around you.

These immersions are an invitation to stay right here — to meet yourself and the moment as it is. They aren’t about fixing anything — they’re about remembering what’s already whole and finding belonging in the richness of now.

What to expect

  • A slow-paced, gentle time in nature (often including a walk)
  • Frequent pauses to breathe, notice, reflect, and reconnect
  • Opportunities for quiet, inner listening as well as optional shared reflection
  • An opening and closing circle, to honor the moment and each other
  • A welcoming space for all bodies, all emotions, and all levels of experience

What to bring

  • A small backpack or bag
  • A water bottle
  • A raincoat or layer in case of shifting weather
  • Good walking shoes (not fancy, just something you’re comfortable in)
  • Optional: a journal, something small and meaningful to carry, or a small mat/sit pad for circle time

Who this is for

You. Especially if you're feeling raw, scattered, inspired, burnt out, grieving, curious, or just want to be around others in a real and grounded way.

You don’t need to be “into mindfulness.” You don’t need to have a good reason. Just come. Be. See what happens when we walk without needing to go anywhere.

Accessibility & energy

This offering is designed to be accessible and restorative. There will be moments of stillness, gentle movement, and optional expression. It’s ideal for people looking for a soft, grounding re-entry into the natural world and themselves.

About Heart of Yoga

Heart of Yoga classes are an invitation to being with exactly how we are in the moment. Movements are breath-led and the sequence is just a guide, accessible to all body types and ages. Another intention for this practice is allowing students to create their own practice and a new type of intimacy with themselves.

“The Heart of Yoga” refers primarily to the book written in 1999 by T. K. V. Desikachar, son of yogi Sri Tirumalai Krishnamacharya, and later to the Heart of Yoga Association founded by Mark Whitwell (my teacher’s teacher), a direct friend of Desikachar and student of Krishnamacharya. The lineage emphasizes Viniyoga – the development of personalized practices to the individual’s physical and mental needs -, breath-centered practices making it accessible to all, and philosophical depth by following Patanjali’s Yoga Sutras and preserving the Yoga tradition.

Restorative & Yoga Nidra classes are a slower pace practice, inviting the students to stay in an asana (Yoga posture) for 2 to 3 minutes, slowing their breath rhythm and using props to aid relaxation, stretch and restauration of tissues. Yoga Nidra is a guided meditation practice in which the mind state is in between wakefulness and sleep, inducing the activation of the parasympathetic nervous system, responsible for rest and digest functions and regulation of hormones that promote the sense of safety and availability for connection.

About

ConcordiaSpring is a multidimensional collaborative project focused on building somatic awareness, communal living, and supporting human development. Founded in 2026 by Christopher Hunt and Brunna Belfiore, the project was born from a desire to live in devotion to love and the larger aspirations of life: service, reciprocity, and renewal.

Christopher Hunt
Coach and Facilitator
Brunna Belfiore
Coach and Yoga Instructor
"Presence grows like a tree that finds far reaches of canopy and the kiss and warmth of the sun by being so devoted to the soil that it roots deep into stable ground, pulls up clean water and dense minerals, and expands into such beauty and elegance, sharing space and making room for the brethren that surround it at the heights of the overstory."

Brunna landed in Asheville...

...NC in June 2025, after travelling the world for 6 years. She left her birth country, Brasil, to pursue a passion for cooking, which led her to many other passions, such as hiking, being close to water, Yoga and mindfulness, Contact Improvisation, and Coaching. She went where my passions and curiosities led her.

Initially, traveling was a pursuit of her passions and being in nature, which had become a safe “place”, the place she felt most comfortable in, enjoying being alone. Travel turned out to be more about the people she met along the way. It was with people that she felt most connected and seen, it was with people that she was mirrored and had opportunities to get to know herself more. Finally, one day, someone literally told her: “It is all about the people!”, and he was right.

Brunna also encountered the desire to find “home” inside and out. She went back to Brasil for a year, travelling to attractive places – where nature and community meet. Finding home inside herself, but still missed out on the feeling of belonging. Then, she heard a calling to return to Koh Tao, a special island in Thailand that she’d been to several times before.

A calling to commit to a decision; to settle in a place and allow that to become home. A decision that led her to meet Christopher, her best friend, husband, life and business partner.

After several years...

...of international travel, asking the universe questions about relationships and connections, place and shared meaning, Christopher found something new, something full of hope and awe, in the vibrancy and aliveness of the ocean. The journey into teaching freediving was a natural one, as it opened doors back into his body through breathwork, meditation, and body awareness practices, doors that had remained stubbornly closed up until that point.

Since then, Christopher has focused on softening into a heart of presence through yoga practices, meditation, and writing, to bring the support, potency, and vision of stillness and listening into all relationships and encounters.

Coming back to the United States was a choice of reintegration, and the desire to share the boon of what he was given on his journey stands tall as a lasting intention in all the work he does. Coaching and somatic facilitation are a limb of that aspiration.

We share many values and have been learning with each other the deepest meanings of being in relationship, not just with each other, but with nature, with presence, and the larger body of life.
